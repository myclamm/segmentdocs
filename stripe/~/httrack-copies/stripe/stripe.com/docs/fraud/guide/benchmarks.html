Benchmark Dispute Levels for Online Businesses

To give you an idea of what an average loss rate looks like for each different business type, here is some aggregated data from businesses using Stripe. This is part four of our Understanding and Preventing Online Fraud guide.

You can use the following information to determine what your fraud losses might reasonably be for your own business. It’s important to note that this is just a guide—your own dispute rates may end up being very different, especially if you’re processing a low volume of orders.

Retail

Retail businesses can be a target for fraudsters who hope to have the goods shipped to them before the seller receives the dispute, with the goal of reselling the items. Products that are in high demand and are easy to resell will attract more fraudsters, especially if the goods are expensive.

The good news is that the majority of retail businesses don’t face significant fraud–the median dispute level is around 0.02%. However, the top 1% of businesses most affected by fraud see dispute rates in excess of 4%, underlining the importance of managing fraud risk carefully.

Software and digital content

Software companies see a median dispute rate of around 0.04%, indicating that the majority of businesses do not encounter too much fraud. The top 1% of businesses see dispute rates in excess of 2%, showing that the businesses at the top end of the spectrum do see dispute rates that are higher than they need to be in the long term.

One structural consideration for combatting fraud in the software and content industry is revocable licensing. If you’re able to revoke access to the purchased software or content in the event of a dispute, you’re able to significantly lower the potential returns available to the fraudster via resale. Any digital content related to gambling is at a particularly high risk.

Services

Services businesses generally experience a low dispute rate of under 0.1%–a low fraud rate for the majority of businesses in this area. However, the top 1% of businesses have a dispute rate in excess of 4%, which indicates a substantial risk at the high end of the spectrum. Since the services category is so broad, it’s difficult to offer specific advice. In general, you should focus on the dynamics of your own business, as well as the types of customer you typically attract.

Ticketing and events

The ticketing and events industry has a relatively low dispute rate compared to other industries with a median of under 0.1%. The top 1% of businesses have a dispute rate of just over 1%, which is comparatively low. There are favorable structural considerations: if a charge is disputed for a ticket purchase prior to the event, the business can simply cancel the ticket.

Education

The median dispute rate is about 0.01% in the education category. This indicates that for most businesses in this category, disputes are not an issue, though the dispute rate is rather high for a small subset of businesses: the top 1% of businesses see a dispute rate of over 1%. Even for those businesses at the very top end of the range, the dispute rate is generally quite manageable. A prolonged dispute rate above 1% may be flagged by a credit card network which can result in a business being monitored, so it’s important to work to keep that dispute rate below this.

Financial services

Businesses within this category see median dispute rate of around 0.02%. This indicates that for most businesses in this category disputes are not an issue, though the dispute rate is rather high for a small subset: the top 1% of businesses see a dispute rate in excess of 7%.

This is a bigger issue for businesses at the top end of the range. A dispute rate of over 7% will certainly attract the attention of the card networks, likely landing the company on a monitoring program with the associated (and steep!) penalty fee.

Nonprofit organizations

Unfortunately, nonprofits can find themselves the target of fraud. Nonprofits who offer supporters the option to specify any donation amount on their site can be targeted by fraudsters that make use of stolen credit card information to test whether the cards still work. This is seen by very low donation amounts or whether the owner has already alerted the bank and had the card canceled.

This can be a serious issue for the business due to the fixed penalty fee associated with each disputed charge. The fees from tens or hundred of disputes can really add up, even if the amount of the fraud is not large. In line with this, median dispute rate is under 0.1%, with the top 1% of non-profits seeing disputes rates of around 0.6%.

Since every dispute counts toward a business’ overall dispute rate, regardless of whether they are won or lost, the rate can soar quickly if this type of fraud is not immediately spotted and remedied.

Travel

The travel industry sees a median dispute rate of around 0.01%–a little higher than other industries, but still quite a low and manageable rate.The top 1% of businesses see a dispute rate in excess of 6%, which is unsustainable. If your’re seeing dispute rates this high, you should take remedial action urgently.
